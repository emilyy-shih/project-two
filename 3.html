FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Even though the planet is warming overall, some locations can be cooling off due to local factors such as increased cloud cover, precipitation, and the level of aerosols in the air. Therefore, many scientists prefer the term "climate change" to indicate broad changes occurring throughout the world.

According to the National Climate Assessment, 97% of scientists agree that the world is undoubtedly becoming warmer over the past 50 years. However, there may be some disagreeing opinions on the scientific method developed by many scientsits, which primarily occur in the peer review process.

Climate change can drastically alter an area's atmospheric circulation and weather patterns, such as the duration, frequency, and location of droughts, floods, wildfires, hurricanes, and other weather extremes.
